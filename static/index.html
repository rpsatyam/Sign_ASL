Understanding Sign Languages

ğŸ‡ºğŸ‡¸

American Sign Language (ASL)

ASL is the predominant sign language used by Deaf communities in the United States and parts of Canada. It's a complete, natural language with its own grammar and syntax, distinct from English.

  • Users: Over 500,000 people in North America
  • Structure: Uses hand shapes, facial expressions, and body movements
  • Grammar: Differs from English with unique word order and syntax
  • History: Developed in the early 19th century
ğŸ‡®ğŸ‡³

Indian Sign Language (ISL)

ISL is the primary sign language used by the Deaf community in India. It has regional variations and incorporates cultural elements unique to the Indian subcontinent.

  • Users: Over 2.5 million Deaf individuals in India
  • Structure: Combines hand gestures with facial expressions
  • Variations: Regional dialects across different Indian states
  • Recognition: Officially recognized as a distinct language

Why Sign Language Translation Matters
